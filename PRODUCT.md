# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary audience is technical peers, engineering leaders, AI platform and security practitioners, and people evaluating Jeremy London's work. They are usually reading in a professional context: checking judgment, depth, taste, and practical experience across AI systems, distributed infrastructure, security, and engineering leadership.

## Product Purpose

This site is Jeremy London's personal website, technical blog, and public work surface. It gives visitors a clear view of his engineering leadership, production systems experience, AI platform work, security background, reliability instincts, and hands-on technical interests.

Success means a visitor quickly understands what Jeremy works on, trusts the practical depth behind the writing, and can choose a sensible next step: read a post, inspect the work, subscribe, or get in touch.

## Positioning

Practical engineering judgment at the intersection of AI systems, security, reliability, and team leadership.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: get in touch with Jeremy; read the blog, follow GitHub or LinkedIn, or subscribe by RSS.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: Jeremy builds teams and production systems around AI, security, and distributed infrastructure.
- Belief ladder: Jeremy has operated in real production and leadership contexts; the writing is specific enough to be useful; his judgment connects architecture, quality, security, and team execution; contacting or following him is worth the reader's time.
- Proof on hand: experience and project details in the About page; technical posts and interactive examples in the blog; public GitHub, LinkedIn, and RSS links.

## Brand Personality

The voice is direct, technical, and pragmatic. It should sound like a capable engineer in a useful technical conversation: specific, plainspoken, mildly dry where appropriate, and allergic to hype.

The emotional goal is calm confidence. The site should feel credible, readable, and lived-in, with enough personality to show that the person behind it builds real things and thinks carefully about tradeoffs.

## Anti-references

This should not feel like a promotional thought-leader site, a generic SaaS landing page, a hype-heavy AI brand, or a decorative portfolio that makes the writing harder to read. Avoid inflated claims, trend-chasing visual treatments, over-polished corporate copy, and design choices that flatten Jeremy into a category.

## Design Principles

- Put the writing first. The interface should make technical notes and long-form reading easy to scan, read, and return to.
- Show judgment through specifics. Prefer concrete systems, constraints, and tradeoffs over broad claims about expertise.
- Keep personality in the details. Small touches can carry voice, but they should not compete with clarity.
- Make technical depth approachable. Pages should reward expert readers without making casual visitors feel lost.
- Preserve quiet credibility. The site can be distinctive, but it should not feel like it is selling too hard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, focus visibility, semantics, and long-form readability. Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep dark and light themes readable, and avoid color-only meaning in navigation, labels, and interactive examples.
