AI can generate interface pieces quickly, which makes design-system boundaries more important rather than less. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating AI ui like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For AI ui, the important part is AI can generate interface pieces quickly, which makes design-system boundaries more important rather than less. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: tokens, component contracts, naming, and handoff.

## The Risk

The bad version of AI ui is familiar: the UI looks consistent while the underlying decisions drift apart. For AI ui, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For AI ui, I would ask for the named decision a designer and engineer can both point to. The AI ui post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the AI ui claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
