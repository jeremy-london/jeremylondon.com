## What Changes

For security, I would start from the constraint, not the announcement. The constraint is Authentication says who showed up. Authorization decides whether the app respects what belongs to them, and the surrounding question is how authorization, validation, dependencies, audit trails, and risk changes in practice.

## Where It Fails

The thing I would watch for with security is quieter than a crash: the happy path works while the unsafe path stays unnamed. That is the kind of break that makes a security post feel padded even when the topic is legitimate.

## The Check

My bar for security is the place where permission, evidence, or ownership is checked. If the security post cannot show that, it should stay short and honest.

That is where I would leave it. Keep the security claim narrow, make the tradeoff visible, and let the example do the work.
