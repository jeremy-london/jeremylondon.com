Agent demos were already impressive in 2023, but the missing habit was measuring whether the system got better. The point is not to make evals sound bigger than it is. It is to name the decision it changes.

## The Practical Part

Evals gets interesting when it changes a real workflow. The example I would use is a team arguing about whether the model improved or merely changed, because it gives the reader something to inspect instead of a broad claim to accept.

## The Edge Case

The failure mode for evals is the explanation sounds plausible while the actual decision stays vague. For evals, that can look fine from a distance, so the page ships, the demo runs, or the team agrees with itself while the next person still cannot tell what actually happened.

## What I Want To See

The check for evals is the example or constraint that makes the point inspectable. For evals, that is enough to separate a useful note from a long one.

I do not want evals to sound more certain than the evidence allows. Make the decision visible, then stop.
