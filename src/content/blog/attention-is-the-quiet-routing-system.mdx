This post is about model routing, context routing, and why production AI systems need more than one path. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating attention like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For attention, the important part is a request that has more than one plausible path through the system. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: routing, inference, latency, and local constraints.

## The Risk

The bad version of attention is familiar: the model choice looks isolated even though the surrounding system made the decision. For attention, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For attention, I would ask for the operational reason for choosing one path over another. The attention post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the attention claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
