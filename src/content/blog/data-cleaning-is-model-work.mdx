Better data often improves AI systems more than another round of prompt tweaks. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating data cleaning like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For data cleaning, the important part is a model choice that changes the surrounding workflow. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: constraints, examples, and maintenance.

## The Risk

The bad version of data cleaning is familiar: the explanation sounds plausible while the actual decision stays vague. For data cleaning, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For data cleaning, I would ask for the example or constraint that makes the point inspectable. The data cleaning post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the data cleaning claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
