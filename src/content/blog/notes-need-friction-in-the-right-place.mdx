Fast capture is useful, but the real value comes from making review and reuse easy enough to happen.

I would keep notes small here. The useful part is Fast capture is useful, but the real value comes from making review and reuse easy enough to happen. That is enough material for a notes note, not a manifesto.

The notes mistake is treating the observation as if it needs a complete theory around it. In practice, personal systems work usually gets better when the first version names the boundary and then stops. For notes, that boundary is how much friction belongs at capture time versus review time.

The notes check I would use is simple: can the next person see what changed and why it matters? If the answer is yes, the notes post has done its job. If the answer is no, adding three more notes paragraphs will not fix it.

The thing I would not add to notes is a fake architecture section. A short notes note can still be technical if it names the pressure clearly: who is acting, what state is being touched, and what evidence would make the claim falsifiable. That is enough for notes. The rest of notes can wait until there is a real example to inspect.

I am leaving notes as a short note because the idea is useful at this size. A longer notes version would need a real system diagram, a failure example, and evidence. Without those, more length would just be decoration.
