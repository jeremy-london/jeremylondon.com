Liquid Foundation Models made small language models feel less like compressed LLMs and more like a different design bet.

I would keep liquid AI small here. The useful part is Liquid Foundation Models made small language models feel less like compressed LLMs and more like a different design bet. That is enough material for a liquid AI note, not a manifesto.

The liquid AI mistake is treating the observation as if it needs a complete theory around it. In practice, model watch work usually gets better when the first version names the boundary and then stops. For liquid AI, that boundary is what changed in the model versus what changed in the product surface.

The liquid AI check I would use is simple: can the next person see what changed and why it matters? If the answer is yes, the liquid AI post has done its job. If the answer is no, adding three more liquid AI paragraphs will not fix it.

The thing I would not add to liquid AI is a fake architecture section. A short liquid AI note can still be technical if it names the pressure clearly: who is acting, what state is being touched, and what evidence would make the claim falsifiable. That is enough for liquid AI. The rest of liquid AI can wait until there is a real example to inspect.

I am leaving liquid AI as a short note because the idea is useful at this size. A longer liquid AI version would need a real system diagram, a failure example, and evidence. Without those, more length would just be decoration.
