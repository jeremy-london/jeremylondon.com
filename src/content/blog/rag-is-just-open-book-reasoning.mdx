Retrieval augmented generation depends less on a clever prompt and more on reliably finding the right context. The point is not to make retrieval sound bigger than it is. It is to name the decision it changes.

## The Practical Part

Retrieval gets interesting when it changes a real workflow. The example I would use is a team arguing about whether the model improved or merely changed, because it gives the reader something to inspect instead of a broad claim to accept.

## The Edge Case

The failure mode for retrieval is the explanation sounds plausible while the actual decision stays vague. For retrieval, that can look fine from a distance, so the page ships, the demo runs, or the team agrees with itself while the next person still cannot tell what actually happened.

## What I Want To See

The check for retrieval is the example or constraint that makes the point inspectable. For retrieval, that is enough to separate a useful note from a long one.

I do not want retrieval to sound more certain than the evidence allows. Make the decision visible, then stop.
