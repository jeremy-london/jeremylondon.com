Package choices affect security, maintenance, performance, and how quickly a team can patch. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating dependencies like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For dependencies, the important part is a model choice that changes the surrounding workflow. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: authorization, validation, dependencies, audit trails, and risk.

## The Risk

The bad version of dependencies is familiar: the happy path works while the unsafe path stays unnamed. For dependencies, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For dependencies, I would ask for the place where permission, evidence, or ownership is checked. The dependencies post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the dependencies claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
