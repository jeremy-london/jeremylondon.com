Production AI needs logs, traces, prompt records, retrieval records, and examples that can become evals. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating observability like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For observability, the important part is Production AI needs logs, traces, prompt records, retrieval records, and examples that can become evals. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: constraints, examples, and maintenance.

## The Risk

The bad version of observability is familiar: the explanation sounds plausible while the actual decision stays vague. For observability, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For observability, I would ask for the example or constraint that makes the point inspectable. The observability post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the observability claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
