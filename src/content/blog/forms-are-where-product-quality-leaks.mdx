Form quality shows up in pending, failed, duplicate, and partially valid states more than in the first screenshot. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating forms like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For forms, the important part is a user trying to recover from a mistake in a form. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: state, handoff, forms, product quality, and boring edge cases.

## The Risk

The bad version of forms is familiar: the feature works in the happy path but leaves the user guessing at the boundary. For forms, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For forms, I would ask for the state the interface needs to make visible. The forms post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the forms claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
