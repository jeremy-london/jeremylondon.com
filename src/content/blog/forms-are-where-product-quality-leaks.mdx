Form quality shows up in pending, failed, duplicate, and partially valid states more than in the first screenshot.

I would keep forms small here. The useful part is Form quality shows up in pending, failed, duplicate, and partially valid states more than in the first screenshot. That is enough material for a forms note, not a manifesto.

The forms mistake is treating the observation as if it needs a complete theory around it. In practice, product engineering work usually gets better when the first version names the boundary and then stops. For forms, that boundary is what the interface must make visible before the user can act.

The forms check I would use is simple: can the next person see what changed and why it matters? If the answer is yes, the forms post has done its job. If the answer is no, adding three more forms paragraphs will not fix it.

The thing I would not add to forms is a fake architecture section. A short forms note can still be technical if it names the pressure clearly: who is acting, what state is being touched, and what evidence would make the claim falsifiable. That is enough for forms. The rest of forms can wait until there is a real example to inspect.

I am leaving forms as a short note because the idea is useful at this size. A longer forms version would need a real system diagram, a failure example, and evidence. Without those, more length would just be decoration.
