Google Gemma and Microsoft Phi-3 pushed small open models into the product conversation instead of leaving them as demo hardware toys. The useful version is practical: a model choice that changes the surrounding workflow.

## What Changes

For gemma, I would start from the constraint, not the announcement. The constraint is a model choice that changes the surrounding workflow, and the surrounding question is how model launches, routing changes, safety posture, and builder impact changes in practice.

## Where It Fails

The thing I would watch for with gemma is quieter than a crash: the release sounds important without changing any concrete decision. That is the kind of break that makes a gemma post feel padded even when the topic is legitimate.

## The Check

My bar for gemma is the new constraint a builder has to account for. If the gemma post cannot show that, it should stay short and honest.

That is where I would leave it. Keep the gemma claim narrow, make the tradeoff visible, and let the example do the work.
