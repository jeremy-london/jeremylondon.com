The quality of a form shows up in loading, empty, invalid, and submitted states, not just the first screenshot. The useful version is practical: a user trying to recover from a mistake in a form.

## What Changes

For forms, I would start from the constraint, not the announcement. The constraint is a user trying to recover from a mistake in a form, and the surrounding question is how content, forms, feeds, frontend behavior, and small-site maintenance changes in practice.

## Where It Fails

The thing I would watch for with forms is quieter than a crash: the page renders but the reader, crawler, or maintainer gets the wrong contract. That is the kind of break that makes a forms post feel padded even when the topic is legitimate.

## The Check

My bar for forms is the browser-visible behavior that proves the page still works. If the forms post cannot show that, it should stay short and honest.

That is where I would leave it. Keep the forms claim narrow, make the tradeoff visible, and let the example do the work.
