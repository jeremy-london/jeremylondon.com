A practical note on using smaller models for focused jobs instead of sending every request to the largest model. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating distillation like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For distillation, the important part is a model choice that changes the surrounding workflow. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: routing, inference, latency, and local constraints.

## The Risk

The bad version of distillation is familiar: the model choice looks isolated even though the surrounding system made the decision. For distillation, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For distillation, I would ask for the operational reason for choosing one path over another. The distillation post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the distillation claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
