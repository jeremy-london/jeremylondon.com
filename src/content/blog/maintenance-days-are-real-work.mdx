Cleaning dependencies, notes, drafts, and small broken workflows is work, even when it does not feel like shipping.

I would keep maintenance small here. The useful part is Cleaning dependencies, notes, drafts, and small broken workflows is work, even when it does not feel like shipping. That is enough material for a maintenance note, not a manifesto.

The maintenance mistake is treating the observation as if it needs a complete theory around it. In practice, personal systems work usually gets better when the first version names the boundary and then stops. For maintenance, that boundary is how much friction belongs at capture time versus review time.

The maintenance check I would use is simple: can the next person see what changed and why it matters? If the answer is yes, the maintenance post has done its job. If the answer is no, adding three more maintenance paragraphs will not fix it.

The thing I would not add to maintenance is a fake architecture section. A short maintenance note can still be technical if it names the pressure clearly: who is acting, what state is being touched, and what evidence would make the claim falsifiable. That is enough for maintenance. The rest of maintenance can wait until there is a real example to inspect.

I am leaving maintenance as a short note because the idea is useful at this size. A longer maintenance version would need a real system diagram, a failure example, and evidence. Without those, more length would just be decoration.
