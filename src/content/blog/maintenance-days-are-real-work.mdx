Cleaning dependencies, notes, drafts, and small broken workflows is work, even when it does not feel like shipping. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating maintenance like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For maintenance, the important part is Cleaning dependencies, notes, drafts, and small broken workflows is work, even when it does not feel like shipping. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: notes, maintenance, drafts, and habits that survive ordinary weeks.

## The Risk

The bad version of maintenance is familiar: the system depends on future energy it has no right to assume. For maintenance, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For maintenance, I would ask for the small habit that still works when the week is crowded. The maintenance post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the maintenance claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
