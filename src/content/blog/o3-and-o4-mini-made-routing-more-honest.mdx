The o-series releases made the cheap, fast, smart tradeoff harder to avoid and better to name. The useful version is practical: a request that has more than one plausible path through the system.

## What Changes

For openai, I would start from the constraint, not the announcement. The constraint is a request that has more than one plausible path through the system, and the surrounding question is how routing, inference, latency, and local constraints changes in practice.

## Where It Fails

The thing I would watch for with openai is quieter than a crash: the model choice looks isolated even though the surrounding system made the decision. That is the kind of break that makes a openai post feel padded even when the topic is legitimate.

## The Check

My bar for openai is the operational reason for choosing one path over another. If the openai post cannot show that, it should stay short and honest.

That is where I would leave it. Keep the openai claim narrow, make the tradeoff visible, and let the example do the work.
