The GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet launches made the frontier feel crowded in a good way. OpenAI moved first in May with GPT-4o, a flagship model built around text, vision, and audio in real time. Anthropic followed in June with Claude 3.5 Sonnet, which claimed performance above Claude 3 Opus while running at Sonnet speed and price.

Those two launches were different, but they pointed at the same pressure: the top tier was no longer only about slow, expensive intelligence.

GPT-4o's unique move was the interface. The "o" mattered because the model was not just answering text prompts with a vision add-on. The demo made voice feel like part of the model's native surface. Compared with GPT-4 Turbo, the improvement was less about longer documents and more about immediacy. Faster responses, better multimodal behavior, and a single model that could sit closer to the user's actual context.

Claude 3.5 Sonnet's unique move was compression. Anthropic put a model above Claude 3 Opus on many evaluations into the mid-tier slot. That was the part that changed my routing assumptions. If Sonnet can beat the old Opus for many tasks, then every product path using the expensive model deserves a second look.

The comparison between them was not clean. GPT-4o felt like the better bet for realtime multimodal product work. Claude 3.5 Sonnet felt unusually strong for writing, coding, review, and careful transformation work. I would not have wanted a single leaderboard to decide between them. I would have wanted a task set from my own product: support cases, repo diffs, screenshots, logs, policy text, and the weird prompts users actually send.

The practical lesson was that "frontier" had started to split into flavors. One model could be more natural in voice. Another could be better at code review. Another could be cheaper at the same quality band. That makes model selection more annoying, but it is a better kind of annoying. It means teams can optimize for the job instead of worshipping one default.

My launch-week recommendation would have been to rerun evals, not rewrite the whole app. GPT-4o should trigger tests around capture, consent, latency, and visual grounding. Claude 3.5 Sonnet should trigger tests around whether old Opus calls can be moved down without quality loss.

The frontier got faster that summer. More importantly, it got less monolithic.
