GLiNER was a useful reminder that not every language task needs a decoder LLM pretending to be a classifier. The point is not to make gliner sound bigger than it is. It is to name the decision it changes.

## The Practical Part

Gliner gets interesting when it changes a real workflow. The example I would use is a model choice that changes the surrounding workflow, because it gives the reader something to inspect instead of a broad claim to accept.

## The Edge Case

The failure mode for gliner is the release sounds important without changing any concrete decision. For gliner, that can look fine from a distance, so the page ships, the demo runs, or the team agrees with itself while the next person still cannot tell what actually happened.

## What I Want To See

The check for gliner is the new constraint a builder has to account for. For gliner, that is enough to separate a useful note from a long one.

I do not want gliner to sound more certain than the evidence allows. Make the decision visible, then stop.
