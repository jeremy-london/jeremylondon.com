Synthetic data can help teams test rare cases, but it should not replace real examples from production. The useful version is practical: a team arguing about whether the model improved or merely changed.

## What Changes

For synthetic data, I would start from the constraint, not the announcement. The constraint is a team arguing about whether the model improved or merely changed, and the surrounding question is how constraints, examples, and maintenance changes in practice.

## Where It Fails

The thing I would watch for with synthetic data is quieter than a crash: the explanation sounds plausible while the actual decision stays vague. That is the kind of break that makes a synthetic data post feel padded even when the topic is legitimate.

## The Check

My bar for synthetic data is the example or constraint that makes the point inspectable. If the synthetic data post cannot show that, it should stay short and honest.

That is where I would leave it. Keep the synthetic data claim narrow, make the tradeoff visible, and let the example do the work.
