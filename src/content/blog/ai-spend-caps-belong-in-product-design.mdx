I would keep AI cost small here. The useful part is As AI platforms added spend controls, it became clearer that budgets are part of user experience. That is enough material for a AI cost note, not a manifesto.

The AI cost mistake is treating the observation as if it needs a complete theory around it. In practice, security systems work usually gets better when the first version names the boundary and then stops. For AI cost, that boundary is who is allowed to act on which resource, with which evidence.

The AI cost check I would use is simple: can the next person see what changed and why it matters? If the answer is yes, the AI cost post has done its job. If the answer is no, adding three more AI cost paragraphs will not fix it.

The thing I would not add to AI cost is a fake architecture section. A short AI cost note can still be technical if it names the pressure clearly: who is acting, what state is being touched, and what evidence would make the claim falsifiable. That is enough for AI cost. The rest of AI cost can wait until there is a real example to inspect.

I am leaving AI cost as a short note because the idea is useful at this size. A longer AI cost version would need a real system diagram, a failure example, and evidence. Without those, more length would just be decoration.
