Conflict resolution belongs in the product experience, not only in the replication layer. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating sync like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For sync, the important part is Conflict resolution belongs in the product experience, not only in the replication layer. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: offline behavior, sync, local state, and private work.

## The Risk

The bad version of sync is familiar: the app works online but loses the user once the network or device state changes. For sync, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For sync, I would ask for the rule for what happens before the server is reachable. The sync post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the sync claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
