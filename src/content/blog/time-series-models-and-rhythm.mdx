I would keep time series small here. The useful part is Security signals often come down to timing, baselines, drift, and knowing when a pattern changed. That is enough material for a time series note, not a manifesto.

The time series mistake is treating the observation as if it needs a complete theory around it. In practice, model systems work usually gets better when the first version names the boundary and then stops. For time series, that boundary is where the model stops and the serving system starts.

The time series check I would use is simple: can the next person see what changed and why it matters? If the answer is yes, the time series post has done its job. If the answer is no, adding three more time series paragraphs will not fix it.

The thing I would not add to time series is a fake architecture section. A short time series note can still be technical if it names the pressure clearly: who is acting, what state is being touched, and what evidence would make the claim falsifiable. That is enough for time series. The rest of time series can wait until there is a real example to inspect.

I am leaving time series as a short note because the idea is useful at this size. A longer time series version would need a real system diagram, a failure example, and evidence. Without those, more length would just be decoration.
