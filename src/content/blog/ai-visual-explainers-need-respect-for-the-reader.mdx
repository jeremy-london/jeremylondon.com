## The Risk

The bad version of AI visuals is familiar: the visual is interesting but does not teach the system underneath. For AI visuals, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For AI visuals, I would ask for the control that lets the reader change the result. The AI visuals post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the AI visuals claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
