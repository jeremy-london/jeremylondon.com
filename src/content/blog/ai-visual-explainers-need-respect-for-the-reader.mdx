AI-generated visuals can help technical writing, but only when the visual teaches something the text cannot. That was the short version in February 2026. The part that mattered in practice was messier, because the change moved the work around and forced the same tradeoffs to show up in daylight.

## why I cared then

A visual explainer fails when it turns the reader into an audience instead of a participant. I would write about it from the point of view of the person who has to keep the creative coding path working after launch, when the launch-week glow is gone and the bugs are still there.

## the maintenance edge

I would keep the point tight. The useful part is the decision it changes, because that is what people have to carry into the next round of work.

A good explainer lets the reader slow down, replay the hard part, and change an input. If the visual only performs the concept, it may look polished while teaching almost nothing durable.
