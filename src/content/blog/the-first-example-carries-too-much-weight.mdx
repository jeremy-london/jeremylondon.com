Readers build their mental model from the first runnable example, so that example has to be honest. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating teaching like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For teaching, the important part is Readers build their mental model from the first runnable example, so that example has to be honest. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: examples, visualization, debugging, and reader mistakes.

## The Risk

The bad version of teaching is familiar: the reader can copy the answer without understanding why it worked. For teaching, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For teaching, I would ask for the mistake the tutorial helps them recover from. The teaching post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the teaching claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
