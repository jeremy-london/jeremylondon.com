Tokens are useful only when somebody owns the meaning, migration path, and exceptions. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating design tokens like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For design tokens, the important part is a design decision that needs a name before it spreads. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: tokens, component contracts, naming, and handoff.

## The Risk

The bad version of design tokens is familiar: the UI looks consistent while the underlying decisions drift apart. For design tokens, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For design tokens, I would ask for the named decision a designer and engineer can both point to. The design tokens post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the design tokens claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
