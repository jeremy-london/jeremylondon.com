Running Python in the page changes a tutorial from explanation into a small lab. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating Pyodide like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For Pyodide, the important part is Running Python in the page changes a tutorial from explanation into a small lab. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: examples, visualization, debugging, and reader mistakes.

## The Risk

The bad version of Pyodide is familiar: the reader can copy the answer without understanding why it worked. For Pyodide, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For Pyodide, I would ask for the mistake the tutorial helps them recover from. The Pyodide post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the Pyodide claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
