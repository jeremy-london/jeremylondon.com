Feeds are unglamorous, durable, and still worth maintaining for a personal site. I would keep the argument close to the workflow instead of treating publishing like a slogan.

## Why It Matters

For publishing, the important part is a reader following updates without depending on another timeline. That gives the idea a place to stand. Without that concrete surface, the post turns into category talk: content, forms, feeds, frontend behavior, and small-site maintenance.

## The Risk

The bad version of publishing is familiar: the page renders but the reader, crawler, or maintainer gets the wrong contract. For publishing, sometimes that means a decision with no trail, a boundary with no owner, or an example that does not prove the point.

## My Bar

For publishing, I would ask for the browser-visible behavior that proves the page still works. The publishing post can be brief and still be useful if that evidence is present.

The smaller version is stronger. It gives the reader the publishing claim, the failure mode, and the check without pretending there is a larger theory hiding underneath.
