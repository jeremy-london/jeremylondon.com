An interactive technical essay should let the reader change the system, not just admire a captured result. That was the short version in August 2024. The part that mattered in practice was messier, because the change moved the work around and forced the same tradeoffs to show up in daylight.

## where this showed up

A screenshot proves the author saw something once. State lets the reader test whether the idea still holds. I would write about it from the point of view of the person who has to keep the creative coding path working after launch, when the launch-week glow is gone and the bugs are still there.

## the check I would run

I would keep the point tight. The useful part is the decision it changes, because that is what people have to carry into the next round of work.

State lets the reader become part of the argument. A slider, toggle, or saved step can show the boundary of an idea in a way a frozen image never will.
